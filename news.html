9 Of The Oldest Structures In The World That Are Still Standing

During the time of the Neolithic Age, which lasted from around 9,000 BCE to 3,000 BCE. During these times the world was extraordinarily different from preset time, In the Neolithic Age, there were ancient civilizations which at the time were just settling into permanent Homesteads of Rock, Which represent the oldest known structures made by humans. These ancient civilizations were also known as Nomads left behind various monuments that were carved with their beings and experiences.

During the time between 3,500 to 2,500 BCE, the Megalithic Temples of Malta are one of the oldest structures in the world. The group consists of Old Temples Older than the Most Ancient constructions. Evidence speculates that the people who made These Ancient Monuments were Local Farmers. Their many different Temples scattered around.

The Knap of Howar is located on the Scottish island of Papa Westray and is home to a Neolithic farmstead dating back to 3,500 BCE. Made up of two adjacent, rounded rectangular, thick-walled buildings with very low doorways, This farmstead is believed to be the oldest preserved stone house in northern Europe. Many things have been discovered in these buildings such as grinders and borers which have been uncovered in other related sites. Religious practices were also practiced in these ancient buildings.
